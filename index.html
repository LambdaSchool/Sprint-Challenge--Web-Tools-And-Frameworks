A Proposition

Hello,

While you may not know it, you have entered a random stream of consciousness of another person's psyche. Namely mine. The reason for this digression is mainly because I want to test the capabilities of the scroll function of this modal and didn't want to just copy paste some random text and deal with the potential of plagiarism. So I'm afraid that you're stuck with me in a way. I'm currently trying to learn Bootstrap in my computer science immersive bootcamp program. There are days that I feel like I'm a fish out of water and that I will never understand everything that's going on and other days where I feel the opposite. On days that I understand what's going on, I try really hard to share that knowledge with other people because I have always been a firm believer in the theory that it is best to teach a concept that you know in order to better understand how well you know the concept. It's the only reason I'm still jacked in to the Lambda Prep Slack - to occasionally help out and try to explain concepts in a way that people that don't know anything about JavaScript may still understand. I wonder if this has been long enough to test the scrolling capabilities of this bar. What exactly is considered legal to copy without repercussion? My cats have settled down for a while, which is nice. They've tried.... I say they, but I really mean Robert Lazarus (Bobcat) ... to eat my class notes again today. I guess that's what I get for being a paper and pen kind of person. It's not that I find it any faster - I'm a touch typist and used to type for a living so I'm actually faster than a lot of people - but it gets in the way of my ability to think. Something about typing just gets me focused on what I'm putting on the page without actually paying attention to what's going onto the page, so if anything it hinders my learning. I don't know why I'm talking about all this on a modal for a page about the moon, but I'm running on the assumption that no one's really going to read this so it's more for my own amusement than anything else. I was tempted to use a poem instead... and I might just. In fact, here you go, one of my favorites.

Go placidly amid the noise and haste,

and remember what peace there may be in silence.

As far as possible without surrender

be on good terms with all persons.

Speak your truth quietly and clearly;

and listen to others,

even the dull and the ignorant;

they too have their story.


Avoid loud and aggressive persons,

they are vexations to the spirit.

If you compare yourself with others,

you may become vain and bitter;

for always there will be greater and lesser persons than yourself.

Enjoy your achievements as well as your plans.


Keep interested in your own career, however humble;

it is a real possession in the changing fortunes of time.

Exercise caution in your business affairs;

for the world is full of trickery.

But let this not blind you to what virtue there is;

many persons strive for high ideals;

and everywhere life is full of heroism.


Be yourself.

Especially, do not feign affection.

Neither be cynical about love;

for in the face of all aridity and disenchantment

it is as perennial as the grass.


Take kindly the counsel of the years,

gracefully surrendering the things of youth.

Nurture strength of spirit to shield you in sudden misfortune.

But do not distress yourself with dark imaginings.

Many fears are born of fatigue and loneliness.

Beyond a wholesome discipline,

be gentle with yourself.


You are a child of the universe,

no less than the trees and the stars;

you have a right to be here.

And whether or not it is clear to you,

no doubt the universe is unfolding as it should.


Therefore be at peace with God,

whatever you conceive Him to be,

and whatever your labors and aspirations,

in the noisy confusion of life keep peace with your soul.


With all its sham, drudgery, and broken dreams,

it is still a beautiful world.

Be cheerful.

Strive to be happy.
